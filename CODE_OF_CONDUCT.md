# Code of conduct

This project exists because people are willing to share their work in public.
Treat that generosity with respect.

## Expected behavior

- Be welcoming to contributors of every skill level. A first portfolio
  submission deserves the same courtesy as a hundredth.
- Give feedback on the work, not the person. "This grid breaks on mobile" is
  useful; comments about someone's ability are not.
- Assume good faith. Most mistakes in a submission are mistakes, not malice.
- Respect that every portfolio in `f1/` belongs to its author. Don't copy,
  rebrand, or repurpose someone else's submitted work outside this project.

## Not okay

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory comments in issues, pull requests,
  or commit messages.
- Submitting someone else's portfolio without their permission.
- Deliberately submitting malicious code (trackers, obfuscated scripts,
  anything trying to break out of the sandboxed preview).
- Spam submissions, or PRs that exist only to farm contribution counts.

## Reporting

If something in this repository's issues, PRs, or discussions crosses a
line, open an issue tagged `conduct` or contact the maintainer directly
through their GitHub profile. Reports will be handled privately.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, close, or remove contributions that violate this code,
and may block repeat offenders from the repository. This is a small
volunteer-run project — enforcement is best-effort, not legal process.
